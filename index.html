About the Founder: The Story of SGT. Evian Boza

Evian Boza's journey is one of resilience, determination, and the pursuit of a lifelong dream. Born in Matanzas, Cuba, he moved to Miami, Florida, at 15. After high school, he built a career in business security integrations, a field that gave him his first taste of the tech world.

After over a decade in the civilian workforce, a divorce prompted him to follow a long-held aspiration: serving in the United States Army. At the age of 32, Evian joined the Army as a 25Q, a Multichannel Transmission Systems Operator-Maintainer. This decision marked the beginning of a new chapter, one defined by service and a thorough examination of network communications.

Stationed at Fort Cavazos, SGT. Boza serves in the 11th Signal Brigade, where he has cross-trained into other technical MOS fields like 25L (Cable Systems Installer) and 25B (Information Technology Specialist). His experience navigating the transition from civilian life to a highly technical military role, and his ambition to earn Network+ and CCNA certifications and degrees in computer science and cybersecurity, became the inspiration for Signal Forward. He envisioned a tool that could help his fellow service members see the clear path from their military training to a rewarding civilian tech career.

Application Prototype: Signal Forward Features

Signal Forward is designed to be a comprehensive career transition tool. Here are its core features:

1. MOS to Career Translator

This tool demystifies the transition process by mapping military roles to civilian equivalents. For example:

  • 25H (Network Communication Systems Specialist): Translates to roles like Network Technician, Systems Administrator, or Telecommunications Specialist. The tool highlights skills in installing, operating, and maintaining IP-based networks and COMSEC devices.
  • 25B (Information Technology Specialist): Maps to IT Support Specialist, Network Administrator, and Cybersecurity Analyst roles. It emphasizes experience with system administration, network configuration, and cyber defense.
  • 25Q (Multichannel Transmission Systems Operator): Connects to careers like Field Service Technician or Satellite Communications Technician, focusing on skills with line-of-sight and satellite systems.

2. Certification Pathway Planner

Achieving industry-recognized certifications is crucial. This feature provides step-by-step guides for certifications like:

  • CompTIA Network+: A foundational certification for networking careers, often recommended for military personnel. The planner details study resources, exam objectives, and information on using military benefits for funding.
  • Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA): A more advanced certification that validates skills in installing, configuring, and troubleshooting switched and routed networks. The app provides a roadmap and connects users with training programs.

3. Mentorship Network

Connect with veterans who have successfully transitioned into the tech industry. Users can search for mentors based on their former MOS, current civilian role, or company. Successful veteran mentoring platforms inspire this feature.

User Guide & FAQ

How do I get started?

Step 1: Create Your Profile. Sign up and enter your MOS, training background, and career goals. The more detail you provide, the better we can personalize your experience.

Step 2: Use the MOS Translator. Navigate to the "Translator" tab and see which civilian careers are a strong match for your military experience. Explore job descriptions, salary expectations, and required skills.

Step 3: Plan Your Certifications. Visit the "Certifications" page to see recommended credentials for your desired career path. Follow our step-by-step guides to prepare for exams.

Step 4: Connect with a Mentor. Use the "Mentorship" network to find and connect with veterans in your target field for advice and networking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this service really free?
Yes, 100%. There are no hidden fees or premium tiers for service members and veterans.

How is my data used?
Your data is used solely to personalize your experience within the app. We do not share your personal information with third parties without your explicit consent.

Can I use this app before I transition out of the military?
Absolutely. We recommend starting 12-18 months before your separation date to give yourself ample time to plan, study for certifications, and network.
